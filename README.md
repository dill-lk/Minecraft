## 🌍 The World of Mayaan

Thousands of years ago, the **Mayaan** — a mighty empire of scholars, warriors, and mystics — built wonders that defied the natural order. Their cities floated above canopies of eternal forest. Their priests could speak to stars. Their engineers carved dungeons so deep that they punched through into other dimensions.

Then, in a single night known as **The Unraveling**, everything vanished.

No war. No plague. No disaster anyone could name. The people were simply *gone*, leaving behind:

- Colossal stone temples swallowed by living jungle
- Runic machines still humming with energy millennia later
- Sealed vaults holding knowledge — and horrors — beyond imagining
- A world now governed by the creatures, plants, and forces the Mayaan had once tamed

**You arrive on this world with nothing but your hands, your wits, and a fragment of a map carved in a language nobody alive can read.**

Discover what happened to the Mayaan.  
Inherit their power.  
Or become the next civilization to be swallowed by the dark.

## 📜 The Story

*For the full narrative, see [docs/STORY.md](docs/STORY.md).*

### Prologue — The Dreamer's Shore

You wash ashore on the **Isle of First Light** — a small island covered in ancient carved monoliths that glow faintly at dusk. Your ship is gone. Your companions are gone. In your hand is a **Stone Shard** engraved with a single Mayaan glyph: *Seek.*

From the moment you step onto the island, the world speaks to you in subtle ways. Animals pause before fleeing. Ruined towers call to you in half-heard whispers. An ancient construct — a golem you'll come to know as **Ix** — emerges from the jungle and silently guides you toward a buried temple.

This is where your journey begins.

### Act I — The Shattered Lands

You explore the main continent: **Xibalkaal**. A patchwork of impossible biomes, floating islands, underground oceans, and crystal deserts, all stitched together by Mayaan ley-lines that still hum with dormant power. You gather resources, build a base, and begin piecing together the story from glyphs, journals, and construct memories you find in ruins.

You learn the Mayaan did not simply vanish. They were **pulled somewhere else**.

### Act II — The Gate of Stars

Deep in the **Serpent Highlands**, you find the remains of the **Astral Gate** — a portal the Mayaan used to travel between dimensions. Shattered in The Unraveling, its pieces scattered across the world's most dangerous places. Rebuilding it means facing the world's greatest challenges: the **Jungle Lords**, the **Warden of the Abyss**, and the **Forgotten King** who guards the final shard.

### Act III — Beyond the Veil

You pass through the repaired gate and enter **Yaan** — the Mayaan afterworld, a dimension of impossible beauty and crushing darkness where the Mayaan civilization continues to exist, frozen in the moment of The Unraveling. Here you must confront the truth: the Mayaan didn't disappear. They were **sacrificed** by their own gods to seal something far worse from entering the mortal world.

Something that is now waking up.

### Epilogue — The Choice

Armed with the full power of Mayaan inheritance, you face a choice that will define the world's fate:
- **Restore** the Mayaan — bring them back, end their frozen purgatory, and share the world with an ancient civilization.
- **Ascend** — absorb the Mayaan legacy entirely, becoming a living god of the new age.
- **Seal** — re-seal the darkness, sacrifice yourself as the Mayaan did, and give the world another thousand years of imperfect peace.

## 🎮 Gameplay Systems

*For detailed mechanics, see [docs/GAMEPLAY.md](docs/GAMEPLAY.md).*

### Glyph Magic
The Mayaan wrote power into the world through **glyphs** — symbols carved into reality itself. Players collect **Glyph Fragments**, combine them at a **Glyph Table**, and inscribe spells onto their body, tools, and structures. Magic in Mayaan costs **Anima** (the life-force of the world) rather than mana — using too much too fast weakens the environment around you.

### The Ruin System
Ruins are *alive*. They react to the player's presence and knowledge level. A temple you visit early in the game will look different after you've learned more Mayaan glyphs — new passages open, constructs become friendly, and puzzles that were impossible become solvable.

### Timeline Echoes
Certain areas contain **Timeline Echoes** — ghostly replays of moments from Mayaan civilization. Step into an echo and you briefly *become* a Mayaan citizen, living a fragment of history. These echoes provide lore, unlock recipes, and sometimes let you change the past in small ways that ripple forward.

### Constructs & Companions
The Mayaan built **Constructs** — autonomous stone-and-crystal automatons that served every role from soldier to surgeon. Many still function. Some are hostile, some neutral, some waiting to be befriended. A loyal Construct companion is one of the most powerful tools in the game.

### Dynamic Weather & Celestial Events
Mayaan ley-lines influence the weather. **Ley Storms** surge with magic energy, powering dormant ruins and making magic more potent but more dangerous. **Eclipse Events** bring waves of shadow-creatures. **Shooting star showers** deposit exotic materials from the void of space.

## 🏛️ Factions & Characters

*For the full faction guide, see [docs/LORE.md](docs/LORE.md).*

### The Four Factions

**🌿 The Rootweavers**  
Descendants of Mayaan botanists who merged with the jungle itself. They can grow living structures, speak to plants, and command the fauna of the Eternal Canopy. They believe the world should be left to heal naturally, without interference.

**⚙️ The Forgeborn**  
Heirs to Mayaan engineering tradition. They reverse-engineer Construct technology, build powerful machines, and believe that re-activating the Mayaan industrial network will bring the civilization back. Pragmatic, brilliant, occasionally reckless.

**🌟 The Star Callers**  
Mystics who maintained the Mayaan tradition of astronomical divination. They read fate in stars, open communication channels across dimensions, and possess partial memories of the Mayaan themselves. Mysterious and not entirely trustworthy.

**⚔️ The Iron Pact**  
Survivors of generations fighting the creatures that moved in after The Unraveling. Hardened warriors who believe the only path forward is to conquer the world's dangers completely before worrying about ancient history. Loyal, fierce, sometimes brutal.

### Key Characters

- **Ix** — Your first Construct companion. Ancient, damaged, and frustratingly cryptic. Carries fragments of memories from the day of The Unraveling.
- **Cenote** — A Rootweaver elder who can read the world's ley-lines like a map. Has lived so long she remembers when the Mayaan were still present.
- **Kaan** — The leader of the Iron Pact, a warrior of extraordinary skill who is hiding a secret connection to the Mayaan bloodline.
- **The Warden** — A Construct the size of a mountain that guards the deepest Mayaan vault. Not hostile. Not safe. Something in between.
- **The Dreamer** — An entity that lives in the Dream Sea dimension. It has been watching you since before you arrived.

## 🚀 Getting Started

### Requirements

- Java 21 or higher
- 4 GB RAM minimum (8 GB recommended)
- OpenGL 4.0+ compatible GPU

### Building from Source

```bash
# Clone the repository
git clone https://github.com/dill-lk/Mayaan.git
cd Mayaan

# Build the project
./gradlew build

# Run the client
./gradlew runClient

# Run a local server
./gradlew runServer
```

### First Launch Tips

1. **Follow Ix.** The construct that appears early in your first game is not an enemy — it's your guide.
2. **Read everything.** Glyph fragments, journal scraps, and construct memory crystals are everywhere. Lore is gameplay.
3. **Don't rush the night.** Mayaan ruins reveal new passages after dark. Some of the best content is only accessible at night.
4. **The ley-lines pulse.** When the ground glows faintly gold, you're standing on a ley-line. These are safe zones, fast-travel points, and magic amplifiers.
5. **You can't die permanently.** Death sends you to a brief fragment of Yaan — the afterworld. You can even find loot there before being returned to the living world.
