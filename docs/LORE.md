## The Age of Formation

Before civilization, before even recorded memory, the world of **Xibalkaal** was a place of raw creative energy. The planet's core is not molten rock — it is a crystallized lattice of condensed cosmic force called **Anima Prime**, the original breath of the universe's formation.

This Anima Prime bleeds upward through **ley-lines** — channels in the earth that carry creative energy to the surface. Where ley-lines cross, Anima pools in deposits of glowing crystal, sparks wild growth, and bends the natural laws of physics in small but measurable ways. The entire ecology of Xibalkaal evolved *around* these ley-lines. Every organism on the planet has trace amounts of crystallized Anima in its biology.

The first sapient beings to understand what the ley-lines were — and deliberately tap them — were the Mayaan.

## The Mayaan Civilization

### Society & Structure

The Mayaan were a tall, copper-skinned people with eyes that reflected light differently than modern humans — a biological adaptation to centuries of Anima exposure. At their peak, their civilization spanned the entirety of Xibalkaal: from the jungle canopies of the equatorial belt to the frozen shelf cities of the poles.

Society was organized around **Castes of Knowledge**, not wealth or birth:

| Caste | Role |
|---|---|
| **Glyph-Keepers** | Scholars and priests; interpreters of cosmic law and glyph magic |
| **Shapers** | Engineers and architects; designers of Constructs and ley-line infrastructure |
| **Scouts** | Explorers and dimensional travelers; navigators of the Astral Gates |
| **Wardens** | Soldiers and Construct-commanders; protectors of the civilization's borders |
| **Root-Speakers** | Naturalists and healers; keepers of ecological balance and living magic |

Governance was a **Glyph Council**: seven senior Glyph-Keepers who made decisions through consensus, guided by astronomical readings and ley-line pulse interpretations. The Council met at the **Axis Temple** — a structure built precisely at the point where more ley-lines converge than anywhere else on Xibalkaal.

### Glyph Magic & Technology

The Mayaan's most profound discovery was that **language and reality are the same thing at the quantum level**. The universe itself runs on a kind of grammar. Their Glyph system was not merely a writing system — it was a direct interface with physical law.

A skilled Glyph-Keeper could:
- Inscribe a glyph that made stone as strong as steel
- Write a binding that prevented any living thing from crossing a threshold
- Compose a sequence that bent light around an object, rendering it invisible
- Carve a preservation glyph that could keep a wound from worsening for decades

The deeper the glyph (the more Anima it drew from the ley-lines), the more fundamental the effect. The most powerful glyphs — known as **Prime Glyphs** — could alter the rules of entire dimensions. Only the most senior Glyph-Keepers ever attempted these, and never without the full Council present.

Glyph magic had one hard limit: it drew from Anima, the world's creative force. Using too much too fast caused **Anima Drought** — a localized deadening of the environment. Ecosystems collapsed, ley-lines went cold, and the area became a lifeless wasteland. The Mayaan were deeply careful with this. Their entire technological tradition was built around *efficiency* — doing more with less Anima, always.

### The Constructs

The Mayaan's greatest engineering achievement was the **Construct** — an autonomous being animated by a fragment of crystallized Anima called a **Core Shard**.

Constructs came in hundreds of forms, each designed for a specific purpose:
- **Wardens**: Combat-designed; heavily armored; carried weapons grown from their own bodies
- **Scholars**: Mobile libraries; their memory banks stored centuries of accumulated knowledge
- **Architects**: Builders with dozens of precise limbs; could construct a building in days
- **Tenders**: Gardeners and ecologists; maintained the ley-line ecosystems
- **Heralds**: Messengers and scouts; could be disassembled for fast travel and rebuilt at a destination

Each Construct's personality emerged from its Core Shard — a slight crystallization of individuality that the Mayaan encouraged rather than suppressed. Constructs were not slaves. They were partners. Many Constructs served voluntarily in what they considered meaningful work, and the most senior ones held positions of genuine authority in Mayaan society.

When The Unraveling came, Constructs did not disappear with their creators. They remain — wandering, guarding, waiting, slowly running down, or (in some cases) going mad.

### The Astral Gates

The pinnacle of Mayaan dimensional science was the **Astral Gate** network: a series of massive portal structures that connected Xibalkaal to five other dimensions. The Gates allowed exploration, resource gathering from other realms, and — eventually — the project that would doom them all.

The Gates required enormous amounts of Anima to operate. The Mayaan solved this with **Resonance Pillars** — structures that collected ambient Anima from ley-line crossings and stored it in crystalline batteries. A single Gate activation drained months of collected Anima.

The final Gate the Mayaan built connected to a dimension they called **The Maw** — a place of absolute entropy. They had detected it through astronomical observation for decades: a dimension where the fundamental drive was not creation but consumption. They opened the Gate to study it. They did not fully understand what looking through that door would mean for what was on the other side.

## The Gods of Mayaan

The Mayaan were not without faith. They worshipped six entities they called **The Architects** — cosmic intelligences so vast that no individual mind could comprehend them. The Mayaan never claimed to understand them fully; they simply observed the patterns the Architects left and tried to follow their implied intentions.

### The Six Architects

**Ix-Chel, The Weaver** *(patron of Glyph-Keepers)*  
Deity of creation, language, and the fundamental grammar of reality. She is depicted as a woman whose body is made of interlocking glyphs. The Mayaan believed she wrote the universe into existence and that human languages are imperfect echoes of her original tongue.

**Kukulkan, The Ascending Serpent** *(patron of Scouts)*  
Deity of travel, discovery, and dimensional space. He is depicted as a feathered serpent winding through stars. The Astral Gates were built in his honor.

**Itzamna, The Deep Fire** *(patron of Shapers)*  
Deity of craft, transformation, and the Anima Core. He is depicted as an old man with hands like forge-hammers. The Core Shards used in Constructs were said to be splinters of his divine body.

**Ah Puch, The Quiet Keeper** *(patron of the dead, feared but respected)*  
Deity of death, endings, and the afterworld of Yaan. Not evil in Mayaan theology — necessary. He is depicted as a skeleton adorned with flowers. The Mayaan held elaborate death-celebrations, believing he would carry them to Yaan personally.

**Hunab Ku, The All-Center** *(patron of the Glyph Council)*  
The abstract unity of the other five Architects. Not depicted in any physical form. Believed to be the meta-pattern from which all other cosmic forces derive. The Mayaan never built a temple to Hunab Ku — the world itself was his temple.

**Camazotz, The Devourer** *(the fallen Architect)*  
The most disturbing figure in Mayaan theology: an Architect who chose consumption over creation. A god who aligned himself with the entropic force they eventually identified as The Maw. The Mayaan feared him deeply and sealed his name wherever they wrote it.

It was to seal Camazotz's influence — which had been growing for centuries, feeding through the Gate to The Maw — that the Glyph Council made their final, terrible decision.

## The Unraveling

The night is called **Ek Balam** — *The Black Jaguar* — in the few surviving texts that reference it. It occurred at a precise astronomical moment when all six major ley-line convergence points pulsed simultaneously: an event that happened once every ten thousand years.

The Glyph Council had been planning for generations. They had discovered that Camazotz was not merely a theological concept but a literal force actively consuming the Anima that held Xibalkaal together from within The Maw dimension. Left unchecked, he would eventually hollow out the world's core and reduce it to a dead shell.

Their solution was radical: **The Great Sacrifice**.

Using the Ek Balam pulse as an amplifier, the entire Mayaan civilization — every living person — would voluntarily convert their biological Anima into a permanent dimensional seal. The combined life-force of millions of souls, released simultaneously, would be enough to wall off The Maw permanently and lock Camazotz behind a barrier that no natural force could erode.

They would not die. They would be *translated* — their consciousness and essence preserved in the dimension of Yaan, the Mayaan afterworld, in a state of perfect stasis. The seal required continuous Anima — living Anima — and the Mayaan would provide it forever, frozen but conscious in Yaan, serving as the living battery for the universe's most important lock.

They chose this.

Every Mayaan citizen voted. The margin was 94.7% in favor. The remaining 5.3% — those who refused — were given the option to leave, and some did, scattered to distant corners of the world with enough knowledge to survive but not enough to undo what was coming.

On the night of Ek Balam, the Mayaan lit a million ley-line channels simultaneously, fed their Anima into the grid, and stepped across the threshold into Yaan.

The world went quiet.

The Maw was sealed.

Camazotz screamed in a dimension no living ear could hear.

And thousands of years passed.

## The Age of Silence

In the millennia that followed, nature reclaimed the Mayaan world with startling speed. The forests that had always grown more aggressively near ley-line crossings — freed from careful Mayaan management — expanded explosively. Within a century, the jungle had swallowed cities whole.

The creatures the Mayaan had kept in careful balance — some bred, some summoned, some accidentally evolved — spread across the continent. Constructs wandered without purpose, eventually reverting to base protective behaviors. The great Astral Gates fell dark without Resonance Pillars to feed them.

But the Mayaan legacy was not erased. It is *embedded*.

- Their ley-line infrastructure still functions, just overgrown
- Their Constructs still patrol, just confused
- Their temples still hold their knowledge, just sealed
- Their glyphs still work, just unread
- Their gods still exist, just unworshipped
- And in Yaan, the entire civilization still *lives*, waiting

The descendants of the 5.3% who refused the Great Sacrifice eventually formed the four surviving factions. Their knowledge is fragmented and distorted. Their history is mythology. They know something profound happened to the Mayaan, but the full truth has been lost to time.

Until now.

## The Surviving Factions

### The Rootweavers

**Origin**: Descendants of Root-Speakers who refused the Sacrifice and fled into the deepest jungles.

**Philosophy**: The Rootweavers believe the world is alive — not metaphorically, but literally. The ley-lines are its nervous system. The jungle is its body. The animals are its cells. Human civilization is a temporary disruption that should cause as little damage as possible. They are deeply opposed to large-scale construction, industrialization, or anything that increases Anima consumption.

**Abilities**: Generations of living at ley-line crossings have given Rootweavers a biological sensitivity to Anima flow. They can feel ley-lines the way others feel the wind. Their magic is organic — growing, binding, calling — rather than inscribed.

**Secret**: The oldest Rootweavers know the truth about The Unraveling. Their founders were among those who voted against the Great Sacrifice and lived with the guilt for the rest of their lives. The Rootweavers' pacifism is partly genuine philosophy and partly atonement.

**Leader**: **Cenote** — an elder of indeterminate age who has been absorbing ley-line Anima for so long that she is partially merging with the landscape itself.

---

### The Forgeborn

**Origin**: Descendants of Shapers who refused the Sacrifice and salvaged as much Construct technology as they could carry.

**Philosophy**: The Forgeborn believe that the Mayaan's mistake was not their ambition but their *sacrifice*. They should have found another way. The path forward is to rebuild — to restart the Mayaan technological tradition, reactivate the Construct network, and undo The Unraveling by restoring enough Anima Prime to the world's systems to function at full capacity.

**Abilities**: The Forgeborn are master engineers. They can repair, reprogram, and in some cases *create* Constructs. Their relationship with technology is intuitive and almost spiritual — they dream in schematics.

**Secret**: The Forgeborn's founding ancestors stole a critical piece of information: the location of the Mayaan's primary Core Shard vault, containing enough Construct hearts to reanimate every dormant machine on the continent. They haven't opened it yet because they lack the Glyph Key to do so safely.

**Leader**: **Tzon** — a sharp-minded engineer in his forties who was born into the Forgeborn but secretly questions whether rebuilding the Mayaan world is truly what the Mayaan themselves would want.

---

### The Star Callers

**Origin**: Descendants of Scouts who refused the Sacrifice and retreated to the highest mountain observatories to preserve astronomical knowledge.

**Philosophy**: The Star Callers believe that the Architects are not metaphors but active cosmic forces that can be contacted, petitioned, and worked with. Through the correct astronomical alignment and the right glyph sequences, they believe it is possible to communicate directly with the Mayaan in Yaan — and possibly to negotiate their return.

**Abilities**: Star Callers are the most skilled Glyph users of the surviving factions, having preserved more fragments of the original system than anyone else. They can also enter a trance state called **Star Walking** — a partial consciousness projection that lets them observe distant events and, on rare occasions, glimpse possible futures.

**Secret**: Three of the most senior Star Callers have already made contact with a presence in Yaan. It claims to be Ix-Chel herself. They believe it. They haven't told anyone else because they're not entirely certain it's telling the truth.

**Leader**: **Ek** — a blind astronomer who has never needed eyes to read the stars. She speaks little, sees everything.

---

### The Iron Pact

**Origin**: Descendants of Wardens who refused the Sacrifice because they did not believe the threat was as severe as claimed.

**Philosophy**: The Iron Pact believes the philosophical debate about the Mayaan's past is a distraction from immediate survival. The world is dangerous *right now*. Corrupted Constructs patrol ancient ruins. Entities from dimensional breaches hunt the living. New threats emerge from The Maw's still-imperfect seal. The only answer is strength, discipline, and the will to defend what you've built.

**Abilities**: The Iron Pact trains the finest fighters on Xibalkaal. They have developed combat techniques specifically designed to fight Constructs (targeting Core Shards) and dimensional entities (exploiting their Anima signatures). They also maintain the only functional network of fortified settlements in the wilderness.

**Secret**: The founder of the Iron Pact was not a Warden. She was a Glyph-Keeper — specifically, the most senior Glyph-Keeper who voted *no* on the Great Sacrifice. She rewrote the faction's founding mythology to hide her identity because she believed (correctly) that if the other survivor factions knew a Glyph-Keeper had refused, they would never trust any knowledge she preserved.

**Leader**: **Kaan** — a warrior who has never lost a personal combat and is beginning to feel the weight of the secrets his faction's history is hiding.

## Cosmology — The Six Realms

The Mayaan mapped the dimensional structure of their universe as six nested layers:

| Realm | Name | Description |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Xibalkaal** | The mortal world; densely physical; Anima flows but slowly |
| 2 | **The Void Shelf** | The space between worlds; geometric, silent, used for travel |
| 3 | **Yaan** | The afterworld; crystallized memory; the Mayaan exist here |
| 4 | **The Dream Sea** | The collective unconscious; reality is liquid; extremely dangerous |
| 5 | **The Star Field** | Where the Architects exist; non-physical; barely accessible |
| 6 | **The Maw** | The anti-realm; entropy; consumption; sealed but not destroyed |

The Mayaan believed that consciousness could travel between realms during sleep (involuntarily) or through specific glyph sequences (voluntarily). Death was understood as a one-way trip to Yaan — which is why the Great Sacrifice was conceptually not suicide but *emigration*.

## Languages & Writing

The Mayaan used three distinct writing systems:

**Glyph Script** (power writing): The direct interface with physical law. Single glyphs can be any size from thumbnail to building-sized. Potency scales with size and the quality of the carving medium. This is what players learn and use for magic.

**Codex Script** (knowledge writing): A complex phonetic-ideographic hybrid used for record-keeping, literature, and science. Requires translation — the Star Callers have preserved the most of this.

**Heart Script** (personal writing): A simplified form used for personal correspondence, emotional expression, and art. The most beautiful Mayaan artifacts are written in Heart Script.

Modern players encounter fragments of all three in their explorations. Learning to read them — through Glyph Fragment collection, Construct interactions, and Timeline Echoes — is one of the core progression tracks in the game.
