## Overview

Mayaan's main story is told across **three acts and twelve chapters**, with each chapter containing a primary quest chain, optional side content, and at least one major revelation that recontextualizes something the player thought they understood.

The story is **non-linear within acts** — you can complete the four faction questlines in any order within Acts I and II. However, certain plot-critical events are locked behind having enough **Glyph Knowledge** (measured by collected Glyph Fragments) and **Faction Standing** with at least one group.

### The Narrative Pillars

The story of Mayaan rests on four interlocking themes:

1. **Memory vs. Truth** — History is not what happened; it's what survived. The player must constantly question the reliability of the information they're given.
2. **The Cost of Safety** — The Mayaan made an extraordinary sacrifice to protect the world. Was it right? Was there another way?
3. **Inheritance** — What do you owe to a civilization you never knew? What do they owe you?
4. **Inevitability vs. Choice** — The stars say the world will end one way. The ley-lines say another. You get to choose which one you believe.

## Prologue — The Dreamer's Shore

### Setting
The **Isle of First Light** — a small island three days offshore from the main continent. Population: zero (visible). Dense forest, carved monoliths, a ruined Mayaan lighthouse that still functions.

### Events

You wake on a beach with no memories of how you arrived. The last thing you remember is being on a ship during a storm. The ship is gone. Your companions (if any were with you) are gone. The sea is calm and impossibly clear.

**First discovery**: A circle of monoliths on the beach, each carved with a single Mayaan glyph you cannot yet read. At the center of the circle is a **Stone Shard** — a fragment of larger stone tablet, clearly broken from something larger. It glows faintly when you pick it up.

**Tutorial**: The island teaches you basic mechanics through environmental storytelling. There are food plants growing near collapsed Mayaan garden walls. There is a collapsed shelter with fixable components. A small fresh-water stream runs through the jungle to the center of the island. The island is gentle by design — this is not a dangerous place. Yet.

**The First Glyph**: On your second night (which the game forces to arrive quickly), the monoliths on the beach light up. The glyph on the Stone Shard you carry lights up too. For thirty seconds, you can *feel* the glyph's meaning — not in words, but in sensation: *movement, intention, direction*. It is pointing you inland.

**Meeting Ix**: Following the shard's pull, you find a partially buried Construct in the jungle. It is approximately human-shaped but made of dark stone and teal-glowing crystal. It has been here for centuries — vines have grown through its joints, and a tree has rooted in the hollow of its chest.

When you approach, it moves.

Ix does not speak at first. It watches you for a long moment, tilts its head in a gesture that is unmistakably evaluative, and then gestures toward the center of the island with one arm before going still again. An invitation.

**The Buried Temple**: At the island's center is a Mayaan temple, mostly underground. The entrance was sealed but has been slowly weakening — your arrival, carrying an active Glyph Shard, has finished unlocking it.

Inside: your first full dungeon. Traps, a minor Construct guardian (not hostile — it blocks the path until you demonstrate you can use the Stone Shard properly), and at the end, a room that is clearly a *receiving chamber* — designed for exactly this scenario. Someone built this as a message drop.

**The First Message**: In the receiving chamber is a **Codex Fragment** — a preserved record left by an ancient Mayaan Scout. Through a Timeline Echo (triggered by the Glyph Shard), you witness the Scout placing this message and speaking directly to whoever would find it:

*"If you are reading this — if the island's lock recognized you — then you carry the Wanderer's Mark. We don't know who you will be, or when you will come. We only know the ley-lines told us someone would. Xibalkaal is in danger again. The Maw is eroding its seal. You must go to the continent. Find the Axis Temple. The Council's final memory is preserved there. You will understand everything when you hear it."*

The Scout looks directly at where your perspective is located — as if she can see you across the centuries — and says:

*"We're sorry for what you're about to learn."*

**End of Prologue**: Ix is now fully awake and accompanies you. A raft-sized Mayaan vessel — still functional, powered by a small ley-line battery — is docked in a hidden cove the temple's map reveals. You sail toward the main continent.

## Act I — The Shattered Lands

### Overview

Act I is the longest act. It establishes the world, introduces all four factions, and builds toward the revelation that the Astral Gate exists and must be rebuilt. There is no single Act I boss — the act ends with an *information* climax: learning the full story of The Unraveling for the first time.

The player's primary goal is to reach the **Axis Temple** at the continent's heart, but the direct path is blocked by four major obstacles, each controlled by one of the factions.

---

### Chapter 1: First Roots

**Primary Quest: The Green Welcome**

You arrive on Xibalkaal's coast in the **Abyssal Coast** region. The landing site is near a Rootweaver village grown directly into a massive ancient Mayaan gateway structure — vines so thick they've become architectural.

**Elder Cenote** greets you personally, which surprises the villagers. She says she has been expecting someone and has been watching the Isle of First Light for decades. She examines the Stone Shard, touches it, and goes quiet for a long moment before saying:

*"You carry a Wanderer's Shard. I thought those were all destroyed. Come inside. Let me feed you before I tell you how impossible you are."*

**What you learn**: The Rootweavers have been maintaining a narrow jungle path toward the Axis Temple's general direction for generations, but it ends at the **Canopy Wall** — a region of such dense, ancient growth that the trees are effectively a solid barrier. Not natural growth. Something is maintaining it deliberately.

**Faction Quest Branch**: If you side with the Rootweavers, you discover the Canopy Wall is maintained by an ancient Tender Construct following protocols from The Unraveling. Resolving this means negotiating with the Construct (possible with enough Glyph Knowledge) or finding its deactivation protocol in a nearby ruin.

**Boss Encounter**: The **Jungle Lord** — not an enemy but a supermassive ancient tree-creature, the apex of the Eternal Canopy's evolution, which attacks when it perceives the Anima disturbance caused by the Canopy Wall being disrupted. Fighting it is optional; communicating with it (Rootweaver route) is faster.

**Reward**: The jungle path to Act I's next phase opens. Cenote gives you your first real **Glyph Teaching** — a structured lesson in the Mayaan writing system.

---

### Chapter 2: The Forgeborn's Gambit

**Primary Quest: The Engine's Price**

The Forgeborn city of **Tz'ikin** is built inside a massive dormant Construct — a city-sized Architect-class machine that the Forgeborn have been slowly awakening over three generations.

**Tzon** meets you in his workshop surrounded by schematics. He is immediately interested in the Stone Shard, which his instruments read as significantly more powerful than it looks. He offers a deal: help him with a critical experiment, and he'll give you access to Forgeborn tunnels that cut weeks off the journey to the Axis Temple.

**The Experiment**: Tzon wants to activate a buried Resonance Pillar — a Mayaan Anima battery — to provide power for Tz'ikin's systems. The pillar requires a Glyph Activation sequence he's been unable to crack. Your Wanderer's Shard can bridge the gap.

**The Complication**: Activating the pillar also wakes up three dormant Warden Constructs who immediately go to *threat detected* mode, interpreting the activation as an unauthorized intrusion. The Forgeborn have never seen active Warden Constructs before — all the ones they've found have been too damaged to restore. These are fully functional, extremely dangerous, and following protocols designed to protect something.

*They are protecting the vault the Forgeborn founders found but never opened.*

**Resolution**: You can fight the Wardens (brutal but possible), reprogram them (requires significant Glyph Knowledge), or — the intended discovery — find that the Wardens will stand down if shown the Stone Shard. They were programmed to recognize it.

**What you learn**: The Stone Shard is a **Warden's Key** — one of a set of seven keys that can bypass Construct security protocols. This makes you significantly more interesting to every faction simultaneously.

**Ix's Reaction**: Ix is unusually agitated during this chapter. They stand very still near the vault door and do not speak for three days after.

---

### Chapter 3: Voices in the Static

**Primary Quest: The Frequency**

The **Star Callers'** observatory complex exists at the peak of the Serpent Highlands — a set of towers built by the Mayaan for astronomical observation and maintained by the Star Callers for centuries.

**Ek** does not greet you. She sends a child to guide you up the mountain. When you finally reach her — in the highest tower, sitting on an open platform, facing away from you — she speaks without turning:

*"The ley-lines have been strange for three weeks. Urgent. When the stone you carry crossed the coast, they settled. You are what they were waiting for. Sit down."*

**What you learn**: The Star Callers have been receiving a signal from Yaan — the afterworld. They initially interpreted it as background interference in their astronomical instruments. Over decades, it has become clearer. Ek believes it is the Mayaan themselves, trying to communicate.

**The Experiment**: Using the Stone Shard as an amplifier (it appears to be specifically designed for this), you and Ek attempt to establish a clear communication channel with whatever is broadcasting from Yaan.

**The Communication**: The signal resolves into a voice. It speaks in Mayaan (which you now know enough of to understand fragments), and it is *frightened*. It says:

*"...the seal is weakening... do not... do not open the Gate until... the key count is complete... we are still here... we can still hold... but the Maw is..."*

The signal cuts out.

**Ek's Revelation**: Ek admits she has been contacted before. The "presence" claiming to be Ix-Chel has been telling the Star Callers to help rebuild the Astral Gate. Ek does not know if she trusts it. She does know the signal from the Mayaan in Yaan sounds *terrified* of something, and they are actively warning against opening the Gate prematurely.

**The Conflict**: Two signals. One encouraging. One warning. The Star Callers are split on which to follow.

---

### Chapter 4: What the Iron Pact Buries

**Primary Quest: Old Wounds**

**Kaan** and the Iron Pact's fortress city of **Xaan Hold** are the last obstacle before the Axis Temple. Unlike the other factions, the Iron Pact doesn't want anything from you — they want you to *leave*.

They have been fighting Corrupted Constructs, dimensional breach events, and the gradual expansion of a region they call **The Grey** — an area of Anima Drought where the ley-lines have gone cold — for decades. They have no patience for archaeology.

**The Forced Alliance**: A major crisis occurs while you're trying to negotiate passage: a **Maw Breach** opens near the fortress — a crack in dimensional space through which Shadow Entities pour. This is larger than any previous breach. The Iron Pact cannot close it alone.

Fighting alongside the Iron Pact during this crisis is the only way to earn their respect. The battle is intense, varied, and reveals that Shadow Entities can be repelled by active Glyph magic — specifically, Glyph sequences that reinforce dimensional barriers.

**Kaan's Secret**: During the battle, Kaan uses a glyph sequence that no Iron Pact warrior should know. You recognize it as Mayaan. He knows you noticed.

After the battle, alone, Kaan tells you:

*"My founding ancestors were not Wardens. The Pact's histories are wrong. My ancestor was a Glyph-Keeper. She left this."*

He shows you a fragment of Codex Script — an original record of the Glyph Council's vote. The margin. The names. The reasoning. And one extra line, handwritten, not in official Codex Script but in Heart Script — personal writing:

*"We had another option. We never tried it. I vote no because I believe we should have tried it first."*

**Act I Climax**: With all four factions now providing assistance, the path to the Axis Temple is open.

The Axis Temple itself is a multi-tier dungeon unlike anything encountered before. Its final chamber contains what the Stone Shard was the key to accessing: the **Council's Final Memory** — a Timeline Echo of the Glyph Council in the moments before The Unraveling, played out in full, answering every question the player has been gathering.

You learn:
- What the Great Sacrifice actually was
- That "the other option" Kaan's ancestor referenced was a theoretical method of sealing The Maw without the sacrifice — but it required seven Warden's Keys working in concert, and they couldn't find all seven in time
- That the Mayaan are alive in Yaan, conscious, and holding the seal
- That something — possibly Camazotz's influence — is actively eroding the seal from within Yaan
- That the only way to help them is to go there — through a rebuilt Astral Gate

## Act II — The Gate of Stars

### Overview

Act II is about gathering the three shards of the Astral Gate's shattered activation crystal — the **Ixchelic Stone** — which were scattered across the world's three most dangerous locations. This act is structured as three parallel quest chains; any order is valid.

Each shard collection involves a major dungeon, a boss encounter, and a deepening of one of the story's thematic questions.

---

### Chapter 5: The First Shard

**Location**: The **Warden of the Abyss** dungeon — the deepest point in the cave systems beneath the Crystal Veins biome.

The first Ixchelic Shard was placed in the deepest vault of the Mayaan mining network by the Glyph Council themselves as insurance — something they never actually intended anyone to retrieve.

**The Warden of the Abyss** is the most ancient Construct in existence: a massive, slow, deliberate guardian of sixty meters tall that has spent millennia going slightly mad with isolation. It is not evil. It is *lonely* and misinterprets visitors as threats.

The dungeon is notable for its use of **Anima pressure** — the closer you get to the vault, the more Anima energy saturates the area, causing hallucinations, enhanced glyph effects, and occasional Timeline Echoes that play unprompted.

**Resolution**: The Warden cannot be defeated through combat alone — it regenerates. The only permanent solution is to show it the Stone Shard (which calms it) and then find its **Core Shard chamber** to deliver a message left in the vault by the Glyph Council: *"We're sorry for leaving you here. You can come with them."*

The Warden, upon receiving this message, goes quiet for a full in-game day. Then it kneels, and the vault opens.

**Reward**: Ixchelic Shard One + The Warden's allegiance (it begins slowly following you toward the surface).

---

### Chapter 6: The Sea Temple's Depth

**Location**: The **Abyssal Coast's** Sea Temple — a massive Mayaan structure submerged at the edge of the continental shelf, only fully accessible during specific lunar phases when the water above it recedes.

This dungeon requires water-breathing tools or magic and is built vertically — you descend through layers of increasingly preserved Mayaan civilization, going deeper into the past as you go deeper physically.

The second shard is protected by **Tidal Constructs** — a class you haven't encountered before, adapted for underwater operation, graceful and extremely fast.

**Thematic Focus**: This chapter is about what was *lost* in The Unraveling that wasn't civilization — art, relationship, everyday life. The Sea Temple was a cultural center: a library, concert hall, archive of beautiful things. The deeper you go, the more personal the artifacts become. Not glyphs of power but glyphs of *love*, of *grief*, of *celebration*.

**Boss — The Tide Keeper**: A Construct that wasn't a warrior but a *curator* — assigned to protect the cultural archive. Its aggression is not military programming but the desperate protectiveness of something that has watched beauty be slowly destroyed by time.

Defeating it doesn't require violence. It requires demonstrating (through glyph sequences associated with the archive's subjects) that you *understand* what is here. That you value it.

**Reward**: Ixchelic Shard Two + the Sea Temple's complete cultural archive (unlocks vast amounts of lore content and Heart Script translation).

---

### Chapter 7: The Forgotten King

**Location**: The **Serpent Highlands** — specifically, a buried city that exists in a pocket of temporal distortion. In this city, time moves differently, and its ruler — the **Forgotten King** — has existed in a loop of the last moments before The Unraveling for thousands of years.

The Forgotten King was the Glyph Council's Warden commander — the leader of all military forces. He voted no on the Great Sacrifice but stayed when the others who voted no left, trying until the last second to find an alternative. When The Unraveling happened, the temporal distortion in his city caught him.

He has lived the same day ten thousand times. He is not mad. He is *exhausted*.

**The Third Shard**: The King did not *receive* the shard. He *is* the shard — his Core Shard (he is, himself, a hybrid of Construct and human, the result of an early Mayaan experiment) carries the final piece of the Ixchelic Stone embedded in his chest.

**Resolution**: This boss cannot be fought. He is at his power peak perpetually, in a loop. The only solution is to break the loop — which requires living through enough of his repeated day to understand what moment he is trapped in and what choice he could have made differently.

**The True Boss**: The temporal distortion itself — the player must solve a glyph puzzle that has been accumulating correct sequences through the King's repetitions and finally reach the configuration that opens a genuine exit from the loop.

**The Ending**: When the loop breaks, the King finally moves forward in time. He is thousands of years old. He looks at you, touches the Stone Shard you carry, and says:

*"I know what you need. Take it."*

He removes the shard from his chest and places it in your hand. He doesn't die — he finally *rests*, sitting down against the wall of his throne room and closing his eyes. The temporal distortion dissipates. You exit into a Serpent Highlands that is now tens of thousands of years older than the one you entered.

---

### Chapter 8: The Gate Rebuilt

With all three shards of the Ixchelic Stone, you return to the site of the original Astral Gate in the Serpent Highlands. The gate's physical structure still stands — the Mayaan built it to last — but the activation crystal's housing is empty.

**The Reconstruction**: A major collaborative event. All four factions contribute what they have been collecting and preserving for generations, coming together for the first time. The Rootweavers provide ley-line rerouting. The Forgeborn repair the Gate's mechanisms. The Star Callers calculate the activation sequence. The Iron Pact guards the perimeter.

**The Warning**: As the Gate activates, the signal from Yaan (the one that sounded frightened) returns, stronger than ever:

*"Not yet. You're not ready. The Maw will feel the Gate open. If you come through unprepared—"*

A second signal overrides it — the one claiming to be Ix-Chel, smooth and confident:

*"Come through. Everything is prepared. Come home."*

The Gate opens. Two signals. One through.

*You choose.*

## Act III — Beyond the Veil

### Overview

Act III takes place almost entirely in **Yaan** — the Mayaan afterworld. This is the game's most surreal and emotionally intense section. The visual aesthetic shifts dramatically: Yaan is beautiful and wrong — floating islands of crystallized memory, architecture that follows dream-logic, light that comes from everywhere and nowhere.

---

### Chapter 9: The Waking City

You arrive in Yaan and find... a civilization.

The Mayaan are here. Not ghosts — *people*. Walking, talking, building, living their last day in an eternal loop, exactly as the Forgotten King lived his. They do not remember The Unraveling. They are suspended in the moment *before* it. To them, you are simply a strange visitor.

You can explore a fully realized Mayaan city at the height of its civilization. This is the game's most content-dense section: every building has stories, every citizen has conversations, every institution has tours. It is extraordinary.

It is also the moment where the game's emotional register shifts from adventure to something heavier.

**Ix's Revelation**: Ix has been here before. Specifically: Ix was built here, in Yaan's eternal moment, and somehow made it back to Xibalkaal — something that should be impossible. Ix remembers *making the choice* to leave and does not know how.

*"I could not stay here," Ix says, looking at the city they were born in. "I knew something was coming. I did not know what. I went back to wait."*

---

### Chapter 10: The Truth in Amber

Deeper in Yaan, you find the Glyph Council chamber — frozen in the moment of the Great Sacrifice decision, preserved as a Timeline Echo so powerful you can walk through it in real time.

You witness the vote.

You witness the Glyph-Keeper (Kaan's ancestor, the Pact's real founder) make her case for the alternative: the Seven Keys method, which could theoretically seal The Maw without sacrifice. The Council agrees it has merit but argues there isn't time — The Maw is already feeding through the experimental Gate, and if they wait for the seventh key, it will be too late.

She proposes a compromise: **Send someone back. Leave the keys. Let a future generation try with more time.**

The Council votes her compromise down by one vote.

You realize you have been standing in the receiving chamber from the Prologue — the one where the Scout left you the first message. She was following this failed plan anyway, without the Council's blessing.

The Stone Shard you've carried the entire game *is* the central connection piece of the Seven Keys method. She built you a path to try what the Council refused.

---

### Chapter 11: The Maw Stirs

The entity called **Camazotz** has been aware of your presence in Yaan since the moment you arrived. He begins actively trying to reach you through the seal — not attacking, but *communicating*.

He is not what the Mayaan mythology said. He is not a god of evil. He is a **cosmic force of entropy** that has no more malice than a fire has malice. He is not evil. He is *hungry*, in the way that decay is hungry. He cannot stop any more than winter can choose not to come.

But he can *slow down*. He tells you this. Not from kindness but from something like exhaustion:

*"I have eaten their sacrifice for a thousand years. It is not enough. I will eat through. Whether you help me or not is irrelevant. But how you respond determines whether what comes after has any... elegance."*

He shows you the truth: the Mayaan sacrifice is failing. Not because it was poorly designed but because *nothing* can hold The Maw forever. The seal will break in a generation, maybe less.

The question is not whether it breaks. The question is what the world looks like when it does.

---

### Chapter 12: The Final Choice

With full knowledge of the world's situation, you face three paths:

---

**Path 1: RESTORE — "The Homecoming"**

Using the Seven Keys method (you now have all seven keys, having collected them across the story without realizing it), you can disrupt the seal *in a controlled way* — not break it entirely but create a temporary collapse that lets the Mayaan in Yaan exist in a state of partial freedom rather than total stasis. This gives them agency to negotiate with Camazotz rather than simply resist him.

*Outcome*: The Mayaan return to Xibalkaal, not as rulers but as refugees and partners. Their knowledge is enormous. Their adjustment to the new world is difficult and sometimes painful. The seal is weaker and must be actively maintained, but now thousands of the world's greatest glyph-keepers are helping do it. The threat is real but manageable. The world becomes more complex, more populated, more collaborative — and the joint Mayaan/human civilization that emerges is something genuinely new.

*The seal holds for another millennium.*

---

**Path 2: ASCEND — "The New Architect"**

You can absorb the entirety of the Mayaan's accumulated Anima — their sacrifice freely given to you rather than the seal — and become a living embodiment of Mayaan power. This is what the entity claiming to be Ix-Chel was guiding you toward all along. It is not Ix-Chel. It is Camazotz — who has been managing his own freedom by manipulating you into becoming a vessel powerful enough to satisfy his hunger without consuming the world.

*The twist*: You are powerful enough to resist him. If you Ascend knowing what you know, you can hold him — not as a seal but as a *negotiator*, brokering a compact between entropy and creation that has never been attempted.

*Outcome*: You become something beyond human, beyond Mayaan — a new Architect. The Mayaan remain in Yaan but are free within it. Camazotz is... not tamed, but satisfied, for now. The world is safe but permanently changed, and its new "god" is you.

*The seal holds for as long as you do.*

---

**Path 3: SEAL — "The Long Watch"**

You can reinforce the seal using the Seven Keys method — not to free the Mayaan but to make the seal exponentially stronger. The Mayaan remain in Yaan. The Maw remains locked. You, like the Mayaan before you, step through to become the new anchor for the seal.

But unlike the Mayaan, you do so *knowing*. You saw the Scout's message. You know this is the old choice made again. You make it with open eyes.

*Outcome*: The world is safe. The Mayaan are grateful but remain frozen. Your companions must go on without you. Ix, who left Yaan once already and cannot do so a second time, stays with you.

The final image: You and Ix, in Yaan's eternal crystal-lit dawn, sitting together in silence.

*Ix says*: "I waited three thousand years for you. That seems like a reasonable arrangement to continue."

*The seal holds for another ten thousand years.*

## Epilogue — Three Worlds

Each ending has a full epilogue that plays out across three time jumps: one year later, one generation later, and one century later. The world changes in response to what you chose, and characters you knew leave legacies that reflect the choices made.

The final scene of all three epilogues is the same: somewhere in Xibalkaal, a child finds a fragment of carved stone. It glows faintly when they pick it up. A glyph on its face means *seek*.

The cycle, whether triumphant or tragic, continues.

## Side Quests & Faction Stories

*Each faction has a dedicated questline of 8-12 missions that runs parallel to the main story:*

- **The Rootweavers**: *"The Last Garden"* — Protecting a specific ley-line crossing that is the last stable ecosystem in the biome; involves negotiating with the Jungle Lord
- **The Forgeborn**: *"The City That Breathes"* — Fully awakening Tz'ikin, the Construct-city, and what happens when the city-mind develops independent goals
- **The Star Callers**: *"The Astronomer's Grave"* — Discovering a Star Caller observatory that recorded The Unraveling in real time, and what they saw
- **The Iron Pact**: *"Blood and Stone"* — Kaan's full personal questline, involving his Glyph-Keeper heritage and a secret about his family's founding that even he doesn't know
